# Scoring Rubric

Use a 0 to 10 scale for each dimension.

## Sonic Fit

- `9-10`: clearly inside the target lane
- `7-8`: strong adjacency with real evidence
- `4-6`: partial fit or mixed catalog
- `0-3`: weak or mostly cosmetic fit

## Underground Credibility

- `9-10`: strong scene identity, real niche credibility, non-generic profile
- `7-8`: believable underground standing with some traction
- `4-6`: emerging but not yet distinctive
- `0-3`: generic or unclear positioning

## Outreach Realism

- `9-10`: direct contact, reachable scale, no major blocker
- `7-8`: workable contact path with minor uncertainty
- `4-6`: contact exists but is indirect or uncertain
- `0-3`: no credible contact path or clearly difficult reach

## Independence

- `9-10`: no visible management, self-run profile, clear direct contact
- `7-8`: likely independent but not explicit
- `4-6`: some representation signals
- `0-3`: clearly managed or agency-routed

## Distinctiveness

- `9-10`: clear identity, angle, or scene-specific perspective
- `7-8`: noticeable personality or curation value
- `4-6`: competent but not strongly differentiated
- `0-3`: interchangeable

## Priority Mapping

Default mapping if the user does not provide one:

- `High`: total >= 38 and no major blocker
- `Medium`: total 28-37 or one meaningful uncertainty
- `Low`: total < 28 or major blocker

Use judgment when a candidate scores well numerically but has an obvious practical issue.
