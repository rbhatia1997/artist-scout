# Artist Outreach

Turn a researched shortlist into practical outreach material.

Prioritize:

- respect for the artist
- clear context
- concise writing
- signal over hype
- genuine fit between artist and opportunity

Avoid corporate or spammy language.

## When To Use

Use this skill when the user wants:

- first-contact messages
- outreach sequencing
- contact prioritization
- notes for why each artist should be approached
- subject lines, DM variants, or short email drafts

Do not use it to invent facts that were not verified in the research phase.

## Inputs

Best inputs include:

- a CSV or table of researched artists
- priority levels
- the label or curator mission
- the specific ask
- any release, event, remix, or compilation context

If the user has not done research yet, tell them to use `artist-research` first.

## Workflow

1. read the shortlist
2. sort by priority and outreach realism
3. identify the contact route for each artist
4. write concise tailored outreach
5. preserve one concrete reason each artist was chosen
6. flag any row that still lacks enough evidence for confident outreach

## Writing Rules

- do not exaggerate familiarity
- do not pretend to have listened to material you have not verified
- avoid fake warmth
- avoid marketing clichés
- keep first contact short
- make the ask concrete
- match tone to the scene

## Default Output Modes

- one-line outreach rationale per artist
- first-contact email drafts
- first-contact DM drafts
- subject line options
- contact-order recommendations

## Message Shape

Default first-contact structure:

1. brief intro
2. one real reason they were selected
3. concise ask
4. simple next step

If the user wants bulk outreach, keep each draft individualized enough to survive copy-paste detection by humans.
